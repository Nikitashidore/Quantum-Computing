Quantum Computing: The Next Revolution In Technology

Introduction to Quantum Computing

Quantum computers use qubits, which can exist in a superposition of states, unlike classical bits. This allows them to perform operations on multiple values simultaneously, leading to exponential speedups for certain tasks.



1 Quantum Bit

Quantum bit is the fundamental unit of Quantum Computing. This Qubit can exist in multiple states at a time, like 0 or 1 simultaneously.


2 Superposition

A qubit can represent both 0 and 1 at the same time, enabling parallel computation and allowing Quantum computers to perform multiple calculations simultaneously.


3 Quantum Entanglement

A quantum phenomenon where two or more qubits are linked, such that the state of one qubit can depend on the state of another, even when separated by large distances. This allows for faster and more complex problem-solving.
